My Story

I built my first model railroad structure kit in 2007, and while it was pretty crude by today's standards, it sparked a passion that has only grown stronger over the years. What began as a hobby quickly evolved into a creative outlet where I could combine my love for history, architecture, and storytelling through detailed miniature scenes.

As I developed my skills, I was determined to share my passion for building easy, realistic scenery on dioramas. I started by doing live performances at model railroad shows and conventions, eventually translating those demonstrations into viewable how-to videos on my YouTube channel, JustanotherScaleModeler.

My approach to modeling focuses on accessibilityâ€”I believe that creating realistic models doesn't have to be complicated or expensive. I enjoy breaking down complex techniques into simple, achievable steps that modelers of all skill levels can follow.

Over the years, I've had the privilege of being featured in MRH Magazine, appearing on Ken Patterson's "What's Neat" show, and even interviewing the legendary diorama artist Michael Paul Smith, whose work has been a significant influence on my own modeling style.

My Approach to Modeling

I specialize in structure models and dioramas, with a particular focus on creating realistic scenery that tells a story. My modeling philosophy centers around a few key principles:

  • Accessibility: Techniques that anyone can learn and apply
  • Realism: Creating scenes that capture the essence of real-world environments
  • Storytelling: Every model should tell a story or evoke an emotion
  • Resource Efficiency: Making the most of affordable and readily available materials

Whether I'm weathering a structure, creating realistic dirt and foliage, or designing a complete diorama, I always aim to share techniques that viewers can adapt to their own projects.

Equipment & Materials

I believe in using a mix of specialty modeling products and everyday items that can be repurposed for modeling. Here are some of the tools and materials I frequently use in my projects:

Tools

  • X-Acto knives and precision cutting tools
  • Various brushes for different techniques
  • Airbrush for weathering and base colors
  • Tweezers and precision placement tools
  • Scale rulers and measuring devices

Materials

  • Woodland Scenics products for landscaping
  • Acrylic paints for structures and details
  • Weathering powders and washes
  • Styrene and basswood for scratch-building
  • Various natural materials for realistic textures
